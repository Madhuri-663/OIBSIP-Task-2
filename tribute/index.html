Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli/King Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India, is not just a cricketer; he's an icon, a legend in the world of cricket. Known for his incredible batting skills and leadership qualities, Kohli has left an indelible mark on the sport.

"The only way to do great work is to love what you do." - Virat Kohli

Kohli has consistently delivered exceptional performances and has been a key player in India's cricket success. He holds numerous records, including being one of the fastest players to reach 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 runs in One Day Internationals (ODIs).

Kohli has received numerous awards for his achievements, including the Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for the ICC Cricketer of the Year multiple times. Off the field, he is actively involved in philanthropy and is known for his fitness regimen and discipline.

2008: International Debut

Virat Kohli made his international debut for India in August 2008 in an ODI against Sri Lanka.

2013: Captaincy

Kohli became the captain of the Indian cricket team in ODIs in 2013.

2018: Test Captaincy

He took over the captaincy of the Indian Test team in 2018.

2020: Fatherhood

Virat and Anushka Sharma welcomed their first child, a daughter named Vamika, in 2020.
